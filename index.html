Fat loss

In order to loss fat the body has to be in a calorie deficit state.
You don't have to eat only salad, you don't have to avoid any specific food and you don't even have to exercise to acheive that.
The most practical way to get into a calorie deficit state is to eat for 3 weeks or a month the same amount of calories each day,
After that, weigh yourself/look at the mirror/put an old cloth and see if there is any change. If you lost weight - keep going
(eat whatever you want as long as you don't change the overall calorie intake and don't decrease the amount of your current activity)
If there is no change try to eat less or (better option) try to change some of your diet to have more low calorie - non dense food.
If you gained fat - do the same thing as if there is no change but to a higher degree.

Cardio

The main importance of cardio is to improve the overall health, and not to (only) to loss fat.
We know now what we have to do in order to loss fat, we understand that cardio is not a mandatory but a way to help us to loss fat.
Incorporate cardio into our life style is very improntant due it's health benefits, 150 minutes of cardio in a week would suffice
and will help you to improve your overall health.

Building strength and muscle

Many think that building strength and muscle are one and the same, but that's not true at all.
Desptie having a high correlation, building each requires a different approach - different workouts.
The workout of strength building should be foucsing on performing lifts of very heavy weight, with long rest time(2-8 minutes) and
performing exersices like bench press, deadlift and squat (and more compound movment exersices).
On the other hand, building muscle will require a lower rest time, lower weight (though still somewhat heavy) and foucsing also on
specific muscule groups like the biceps.

Progressive overload

In order to change our bodies physically - make it able to run longer, jump higher, get stronger, etc.
We have to gradually increase the stimulation of our muscles and our body, this is progressive overload.
So how do we do that? Our body is "smart" so it won't change anything unless it's needed, that's mean we have to "convince"
that the change that we want is important, we do so by taking our body to it's limits (though it's not necessary at all for
beginners). Let's see an exapmle: a person can bench a certain amount of weight for a certain amount of repitions - if he/she
will add slighlty more weight/repitions/sets/etc he will be able to get stronger in time to an uppper limit of course.
